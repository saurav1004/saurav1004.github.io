Faithfulness vs Plausibility :: Draft

Saurav

2025-06-18

In previous writeup on writing I talked briefly about the problem of plausibility vs faithfulness. I came across this core distinction while working with AI systems (particularly LLMs)

There has been quite some research in this area. Here are some key papers that highlight the problem :

  • Chain-of-Thought Reasoning In The Wild Is Not Always Faithful link
  • Language Models Don’t Always Say What They Think: Unfaithful Explanations in Chain-of-Thought Promptinglink
  • Reasoning Models Don’t Always Say What They Think link
  • When Chain of Thought is Necessary, Language Models Struggle to Evade Monitors link
  • The Probabilities Also Matter link
  • Faithfulness of LLM Self-Explanations for Commonsense Tasks link
  • Walk the Talk [link](Walk the Talk)

The core distinction is that faithfulness is a measure of truth, whereas plausibility is a measure of persuasiveness. An explanation is faithful if it accurately reflects the model’s internal decision-making process. An explanation is plausible if it seems logical, coherent, and convincing to a human evaluator, regardless of its truthfulness.

How to solve the Plausibility vs Faithfulness problem ?

One way is to augment the current systems with harnesses that can monitor and control its output. This direction essentially tries to achieve that. The idea is if you can monitor then you can buil metrics around them, and if you can build efficient metrics you can control and steer the model as you with. This might be the immediate answer to the problme. But I feel this patch work cannot last long. And we need a architectural (model or the harness around it) fix to this problem, Something which is more verifiable and deterministic at inference, but still does not get rid of the expressivity of transformer models.

Some recent work in the latent space reasoning makes the problem even harder, as it’s significantly to harder to monitor and control latent chain of thought.
